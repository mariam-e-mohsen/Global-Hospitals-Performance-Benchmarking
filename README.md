# Global-Hospitals-Performance-Benchmarking
This project involves a comprehensive analysis of approximately 2.5 million hospital discharge records

## 📌 Project Overview
 The dashboard focuses on bridging the gap between clinical outcomes and hospital financial performance, providing decision-makers with actionable insights into resource allocation, profitability, and operational efficiency.

## 🛠️ Tools & Skills Demonstrated
- **Big Data Handling:** Successfully imported, cleaned, and modeled ~2.5 million rows using **Power Query** without compromising dashboard performance.
- **Data Visualization:** Built an interactive **Power BI** dashboard using a professional monochromatic blue color palette to ensure cognitive ease and readability.
- **DAX & Financial Modeling:** Created complex measures to calculate overall costs, charges, profit margins, and cost-to-charge ratios.

## 💡 Key Insights & Business Value
Based on the visual analysis of the dashboard:
- **Financial Performance:** Analyzed massive financial flows, revealing **$117.5 Billion** in Total Charges versus **$39.4 Billion** in Total Costs, with an average overall Cost-to-Charge ratio of **0.33**.
- **Top Profitable Facilities:** Identified the top 3 most profitable hospitals (e.g., *Adirondack Medical Center - Saranac Lake Site, Albany Medical Center - South Clinical Campus,* and *Albany Medical Center Hospital*), enabling benchmarking for underperforming facilities.
- **Clinical vs. Operational Metrics:** Demonstrated a direct correlation between the **Risk of Mortality** (Minor to Extreme) and the **Average Length of Stay (ALOS)**, which stands at an average of **5.38 days**. 
- **Departmental Burden:** Highlighted that *Mental Diseases and Disorders* alongside *Significant Trauma and Burns* account for a massive volume of hospital stays, which directs where administrative focus and funding should go.

## 🎯 Executive Recommendation
For hospital administrators, optimizing the Average Length of Stay (ALOS) in extreme mortality risk cases and closely monitoring the Cost-to-Charge ratios in high-volume departments can significantly reduce the operational costs while maintaining high-quality patient care.
